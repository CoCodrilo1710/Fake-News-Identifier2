FAKE News Identifier

About

Fake news Identifier has a handy tool that you can use in order to help in the fight against fake news!


If you ever find any fake websites, you can submit it in “make a submission”. Likewise, if you think a website is trustworthy, just click on “trust this website”.

The question is, how do you know a website contains mostly false content?

There is a criteria which you can use in order to make submissions correctly:

  1. You part of the news and you know for a fact that this is false. This suggests the website is mostly fake.
  2. The website tackles politics and contains opinions of different individuals, as opposed to facts and stories.
  3. The title is clickbait: “10 Tricks for Perfect Skin (You Won’t Believe #4!)” or „The Biggest Tax Tricks Your CPA Isn’t Telling You About.”
  4. The author is anonymous (or extremely well-known): Fraudulent publishers may use a generic author name or omit mentioning them entirely in order to avoid fact-checks.
  5. The article contains grammaticalor typography errors.
There are some places, however, where the article is fake purposefully for entertaining exmples. Such examples are the articles on The Onion
Please only mark websites as truthful or not accordingly, in order to create a useful database for the battle against fake news.
